# Review of the English Translation in Wahl-Jespersen.md

Review date: 2026-06-13. Line numbers refer to the current state of `Wahl-Jespersen.md` (the English paragraphs). Overall the translation captures the gist well, but several passages have reversed meanings, and a few machine-translation artifacts remain.

## 1. Translation artifacts (must fix)

- **Line 227** — *nomim* (= "namely") was left untranslated and a translator's note remains in the text: "called 'nomim' (I'm not sure what this means)". Should read: "Occidental here has a complicated system, namely: (a)..."
- **Line 245** — The italicized inserted vowels *a*, *i* were machine-translated into English "to", corrupting the examples: "*natur to l-isar, americ to n-isar, vir to l-isar, regul to r-isar, possib to il-isar*". Restore the original forms: *natur**a**l-isar, americ**a**n-isar, vir**i**l-isar, regul**a**r-isar, possib**il**-isar*.
- **Line 245** — The cited Occidental word *Facilitar* was translated into English as "*Facilitate*"; it must remain *facilitar*. Also "is not entirely justified copy" → "is **a** not entirely justified copy".

## 2. Reversed meanings / major mistranslations

- **Line 99** — "*k* es **non-careabli**" rendered as "not usable" — the **opposite** meaning; *non-careabli* = "indispensable" (cannot be dispensed with). Also "*kelki* me ha jus usat" means "the word *kelki*, which I have just used (is an example)", not "some I have just used".
- **Line 149** — "**Do** li semblant simplicità ... es bluff": *Do* (= "Thus/Therefore") was misread as the English auxiliary "Do", turning a statement into a question ("Is the seeming simplicity ... a bluff?"). Should be: "Thus the seeming simplicity and regularity in the grammar is a bluff, and all the exceptions, by the thousands, are hidden in the lexicon..."
- **Line 149** — "**sin** minimal grammatical coherentie" = "**without** the slightest grammatical coherence", translated as "with minimal grammatical coherence" (polarity inverted).
- **Line 151** — "abolir e **despermisser**" = "abolish and **disallow**", translated as "abolish and **permit**" (negation dropped).
- **Line 153** — "To **importa** in li lingue sive foren formes, sive exceptional derivationes": *importa* here means "imports/brings in". Should be: "This brings into the language either foreign forms or exceptional derivations", not "It is important in the language whether..."
- **Line 273** — Three errors in one paragraph:
  1. "junter li auxiliare *ha* **ne** con li participie perfect, ma con li tema verbal" — the **ne** was dropped, producing a self-contradictory sentence. Should be: "joining the auxiliary *ha* **not** with the perfect participle, but with the verbal theme".
  2. "(in **Occitan**: *ha amat*)" — *Oc* is **Occidental**, not Occitan.
  3. "Ti metode ne trova se **necú**" = "This method is found **nowhere**" (*necú* = nowhere), translated as "This method is not **necessary**".
- **Line 298** — "**Vu di** que **un sol form** por li pronómines personal suffice" → "**I** say that **a pronoun personal form** is sufficient" — wrong person and garbled. Should be: "**You** say that **a single form** for the personal pronouns suffices, and that a special form for the objective case is not necessary."
- **Line 299** — "Qualmen vu va far in N li differentie in li secuent frase" = "**How will you express** in N the difference in the following sentences", not "What do you think the difference is".
- **Line 301** — "Vor pronómines **possessiv**" = "your **possessive** pronouns", translated as "personal pronouns" (and "Vor" = "your" dropped).
- **Line 267** — "**yo** posse constatar" = "**I** can note/observe", translated as "you can observe".
- **Line 337** — "Angles **ve misa** distinctione inter *his, her*" = "Englishmen **will miss** the distinction between *his* and *her*", translated as "English **has made** a distinction". (Also "skandinaves **ve** ultre regreta" = "will further regret" — future tense dropped.)
- **Line 339** — "**nultem** probat ante N in ti extensione" = "**never** before N attempted to this extent" (*nultem* = never; *probat* = tried/tested), translated as "no one has proven it before N".
- **Line 283** — "usat solim in **dis manere**" = "used only in **this way**", translated as "used only in **two ways**" (*dis* misread as *du*).
- **Line 213** — "li **tre** pregnant vocales *a, o*": *tre* = "very", translated as "the **three** pregnant vowels" (only two vowels are listed). The first half of the sentence is also garbled; it should read: "the use of *e*, as the less salient vowel, in the cases where the ending differs, is preferable and less shocking than the very salient vowels *a, o*."
- **Line 351** — "on evita miscomprendos e desavantajes **combinant cun** prefixe *in-*" = "one avoids the misunderstandings and disadvantages **associated with** the prefix *in-*", translated as "avoids misunderstandings and disadvantages **by combining with** the prefix *in-*" (direction reversed).
- **Line 137** — "me prega vu **dicte** kelki vordes" = "I ask you to **say** a few words", translated as "I ask that you **dictate** some words".
- **Line 105** — "quel in ti casu in fact trova se **necú**" = "which in this case is in fact found **nowhere**" (*necú* = nowhere), translated as "finds itself **unnecessary**".
- **Line 237** — "**N** have du sufixes" = "**N** has two suffixes", translated as "**We** have two suffixes".
- **Line 25** — "ti adherentes del solution ..., **queles postula** modern naturalità e **factic comun** internationalità": the relative clause modifies the adherents. Should be: "those supporters ... **who demand** modern naturalness and **actual common** internationality". The current translation attaches it to "solution" ("which requires...") and drops *comun*.
- **Line 230** — "nek in scripto ni in parlo **accente** vud es facil moyene tu evita ambiguitate": the subject *accente* (stress/accent) was dropped. Should be: "neither in writing nor in speech would **accent** be an easy means to avoid ambiguity".
- **Line 173** — "Por les es forsan avantaje **tu** trova": *tu* is the infinitive marker, not "you". Should be: "For them it is perhaps an advantage **to find** all verbs with the same ending", not "advantageous **for you** to find".
- **Line 381** — The acknowledgment thanks **Mrs.** (Sª) Dave H. Morris, so "through **his** generous subsidy" should be "**her**". Also *covenio* = "the **meeting**", not "the agreement".

## 3. Systematic error patterns

- **Novial *vor* (= "where") repeatedly translated as "because"**: line 99 ("*s* for orthographic *c* **where** it does not have the pronunciation *k*"), line 115 (*orkestre*, **where** F has the same pronunciation), line 127 (twice: "*g* in all those cases **where** one or more European languages do not pronounce a sibilant"; "*gida, gilotine, gitare* and others, **where** the sound [g] is more international"), line 174 (three times: "the endings *-e, -i, -u* **where** they were necessary because of derivatives"; "in all cases **where** no known derived noun or adjective made another ending necessary or useful"; "the question **where** the ending of each verb should be *-a* or another vowel"), line 179 ("not only **where** the clear meaning is the result of an action"), line 200 ("e/a/o words, **where** one has firstly an ordinary noun in *-e*"), line 283 ("omit *tu* even **where** E has *to*"), line 315 ("**whereas** my system permits adding *-m* in the second case"). At line 350 the same *vor* was instead translated as "your" ("in DESk, **where** *ne* in old times was the ordinary negative").
- ***nomim* (= "namely") mistranslated** three times: line 127 ("called *j*" → "namely *j*"), line 198 ("named *-o*" → "namely *-o*"), line 227 (see section 1).

## 4. Minor issues (optional)

- **Line 21** — "li cause" is singular: "the cause that interests us both", not "the causes".
- **Line 57** — *lingue* left untranslated: "a true usable and viable **language**".
- **Line 62** — *quasi* dropped: "comprehensible to every civilized European **almost** without prior instruction".
- **Line 65** — "secure inroad": *inradication* = "firm rooting / taking root".
- **Line 123** — "Li unes es por..." = "**Some** are for the conservation of the historic image", not "The first is...".
- **Line 127** — "(1) *ce, ci* in no language **do not** sound [k]": double negative; *nun* = "now/today" was lost. Should be: "in no language **today** sound [k]".
- **Line 131** — *presc solmen* = "**almost** only" — *presc* dropped.
- **Line 148** — "per derivation de lor radic-paroles" dropped: "regularly forming, **by derivation from their root-words**, the very numerous (*multissim*) international words".
- **Line 174** — *ante longi tempe* = "**before long**", not "at some point".
- **Line 178** — "thema presentic" = "**present theme/stem**", not "thematic present" (same for "thema perfectic" at line 179: "perfect theme").
- **Line 180** — *talim* = "thus", not "among others".
- **Line 197** — *embarasat* is a false friend: "perplexed / at a loss", not "embarrassed".
- **Line 198** — italicized *omni* left untranslated (= "**all** verbs"; the italics are emphasis).
- **Lines 201, 243** — missing closing quotation marks at the ends of the quoted example sentences.
- **Line 203** — "Tum conducte me a *eso*" = "**That leads me** to *eso*", not "Then I came to". Also *esente* = "a (single existing) **being**", not "essence"; and "li longeso de dis tre curti **file**" — *file* = "line/queue", which in English reads as a computer file.
- **Line 211** — *vice* = "**instead of**" left untranslated ("*konfero* instead of *conferentie*").
- **Line 244** — *apu* = "**alongside/beside**", not "after" ("*in-validar* alongside *invalid-ar*"). Same at line 303 ("How heavy *nostri, vostri* would be **beside** *nor, vor*!").
- **Line 249** — *Ceterim* = "For the rest / Moreover", not "Otherwise".
- **Line 303** — "in assimilation a **lor**" = "in assimilation to ***lor***" (the pronoun itself), not "to them".
- **Line 327** — *unesmim* = "**first(ly)**", not "for myself".
- **Line 345** — "litu-slavic" = "**Balto-Slavic**".
- **Line 372** — Note: the Novial closing text says "fro **Novial e Occidental**" while the Occidental text says "de **Occidental e Novial**" (each version lists the languages in a different order); the single translation follows the Occidental order.
